# REF - Optical Ground Station Design Literature

## Summary

This reference note stands in for the optical ground station design literature that would support telescope sizing, receiver architecture, link-budget modelling, and optical alignment choices.

## Key Takeaways

- Optical receiver design must be evaluated through link-budget impact, not only optical quality.
- Telescope aperture affects collection power but also drives cost, mount complexity, and alignment burden.
- Receiver architecture and pointing architecture are tightly coupled.
- Fiber coupling can improve modularity but increases sensitivity to alignment and pointing error.

## Relevance to QOSMIC

This literature supports the telescope and receiver subsystem of the sample vault. In the production system, this note should be replaced by specific papers, datasheets, internal design documents, and test reports.

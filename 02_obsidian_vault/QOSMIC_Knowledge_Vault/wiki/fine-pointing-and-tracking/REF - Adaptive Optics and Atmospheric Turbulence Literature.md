# REF - Adaptive Optics and Atmospheric Turbulence Literature

## Summary

This reference note represents the body of literature on atmospheric turbulence, adaptive optics, beam wander, and fine pointing control relevant to optical ground stations.

## Key Takeaways

- Atmospheric turbulence can disturb optical links through beam wander, scintillation, and wavefront distortion.
- Fine pointing is needed when residual pointing errors exceed the receiver’s tolerance.
- FSM bandwidth should be derived from measured disturbance spectra, not selected arbitrarily.
- Adaptive optics and fine pointing are related but not identical correction layers.

## Relevance to QOSMIC

This literature supports the fine pointing and tracking subsystem of the sample vault. It helps explain why the system may require separate coarse and fine tracking loops and why a high-bandwidth FSM may be needed.
